# Value Added Tax (VAT)

**Current Rate**: 15% (Since 2018).

## Concepts

1. **Output VAT**: Tax charged by the business on Sales. (Money we collect for SARS). **Liability**.
2. **Input VAT**: Tax paid by the business on Purchases/Expenses. (Money we can claim back). **Asset**.
3. **VAT Payable**: `Output VAT - Input VAT`.

## Categories

### 1. Standard Rated (15%)

- Most goods and services.
- Vehicles, Electricity, Phone bills, Clothing.

### 2. Zero-Rated (0%)

- Essential goods (to help the poor).
- Brown bread, Maize meal, Milk, Fruit & Veg, Eggs, Petrol/Diesel, Paraffin.
- *Business CAN claim Input VAT on expenses used to make these.*

### 3. Exempt (No Tax)

- No VAT is charged, and NO Input VAT can be claimed.
- Interest, Rent (Residential), Education, Transport (Bus/Taxi/Train).

---

## Calculations

**Moving between exclusive and inclusive:**

- **Exclusive to Inclusive**: `Amount × 1.15`
- **Inclusive to Exclusive**: `Amount ÷ 1.15`
- **Calculate VAT amount only**: `Inclusive Amount × 15 ÷ 115`

---

## Exam Tips

- **Salaries**: There is NO VAT on salaries. Do not calculate Input VAT on wages.
- **VAT Control Account**:
  - **Credit Side (Output)**: Sales, Purchases Returns, Drawings (Owner taking stock).
  - **Debit Side (Input)**: Purchases, Sales Returns, Bad Debts (Claim VAT back).
- **Ethics**: Claiming VAT on personal items (e.g., family holiday) is fraud.

## Quick Check

1. Is Brown Bread Zero-rated or Exempt? (Zero-rated).
2. Calculate the VAT amount on a price of R115 (Inclusive). (R15).
3. Can a business claim Input VAT on a dedicated "Passenger Vehicle" (e.g., CEO's sedan)? (No, generally forbidden unless it's for car rental/hiring).
