# Preparing the Full Statement

After completing Operating, Investing, and Financing sections, we must balance the statement.

## The Final Reconciliation

| Details | R |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Net change in cash and cash equivalents** | **Usage: A + B + C** |
| Cash and cash equivalents at beginning of year | + XX |
| **Cash and cash equivalents at end of year** | **= XX** |

**Logic:**

1. Sum of (Operating + Investing + Financing) = Net Change.
2. Add Opening Balance.
3. Result MUST match the Closing Balance in the Balance Sheet/Notes.

---

## Calculating "Cash and Cash Equivalents"

This figure is not just the "Bank" account. It includes:

- **Bank (+)**
- **Cash Float / Petty Cash (+)**
- **Fixed Deposits maturing within 12 months (+)**
- **Bank Overdraft (-)**

**Example:**

- Opening: Bank R50k, Petty Cash R2k. **Total = R52k**.
- Closing: Bank (Overdraft) R10k, Petty Cash R2k. **Total = (R8k)**.
- **Net Change required**: From +52k to -8k is a decrease of **R60 000**.
- Your Operating/Investing/Financing sections MUST sum to **(R60 000)**.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Self-marking**: You can verify your own answer. If your calculated closing balance matches the given balance sheet, you likely got full marks.
- **Order of Operations**:
    1. Calculate Opening and Closing Cash balances first.
    2. Calculate the difference (Target Answer).
    3. Fill in the easy figures (Tax paid, Dividends paid, Shares issued).
    4. Work out the hard figures (Operating Profit Note, Fixed Asset additions).
    5. Check if it balances.

---

## Practice Question Strategy

1. **Skeleton**: Draw the full format immediately.
2. **Plugging**: Put numbers from the question straight into the format (e.g., Tax Paid often has enough info right away).
3. **Notes**: Do the "Cash Generated from Operations" note carefully. This is worth the most marks.
