# Financing Activities

This section deals with Long-Term Liabilities (Loans) and Equity (Capital).

## Format

| Details | R |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cash effects of financing activities** | **XX** |
| Proceeds from shares issued | + XX |
| Funds used to repurchase shares | (XX) |
| Proceeds from loans | + XX |
| Repayment of loans | (XX) |

---

## Share Transactions

### 1. Proceeds from Issue

- Calculate the cash received from new shares.
- `Number of new shares × Issue Price`.
- **Inflow**.

### 2. Share Buyback

- **CRITICAL**: In the Cash Flow Statement, we show the **TOTAL CASH PAID** for the buyback.
- Unlike the Notes (where we split it between Share Capital and Retained Income), here we show the full amount leaving the bank.
- `Number of shares bought back × Buyback Price`.
- **Outflow**.

---

## Loans

- Compare Opening and Closing Balances of the **Loan Account**.
- **Increase** = We borrowed more = **Inflow**.
- **Decrease** = We repaid capital = **Outflow**.
- *Note: Do not include Interest here. Interest is under Operating Activities.*
- *Tip*: Watch out for "Capitalized Interest". If interest was capitalized, the loan balance increased without cash coming in.
  - `Repayment = Opening Balance + Capitalized Interest - Closing Balance`.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Buybacks**: This is the most common trap. Students try to split the buyback. In Cash Flow, **Cash is Cash**. Put the whole amount here.
- **Loan Repayment**: If the question gives you the "Current Portion of Loan", remember that the **Total Loan Balance** accounts for both Non-Current and Current portions. Add them together before comparing opening vs closing.

3. Where does "Interest on Loan" go? (Operating or Financing?)
