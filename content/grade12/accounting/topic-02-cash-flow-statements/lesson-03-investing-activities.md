# Investing Activities

This section deals with non-current assets (Fixed Assets and Financial Assets).

## Format

| Details | R |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cash effects of investing activities** | **(XX)** |
| Purchase of tangible assets | (XX) |
| Proceeds from sale of tangible assets | + XX |
| Investment in fixed deposit | (XX) |
| Redemption (cashing out) of fixed deposit | + XX |

---

## Purchase of Assets (Reconstructing the Note)

Often, you are not told how much was bought. You must reconstruct the **Fixed Assets Note** or T-account.

**Logic:**

```text
Opening Carrying Value
+ Additions at Cost (Purchase)  <-- FIND THIS (Balancing Figure)
- Disposals at Carrying Value
- Depreciation
= Closing Carrying Value
```

**Calculation:**
`Additions = Closing CV - Opening CV + Depreciation + Disposal CV`

## Proceeds from Sale of Assets

Do not confuse **Carrying Value** with **Selling Price**.

- The Cash Flow Statement wants the **CASH RECEIVED** (Selling Price).
- **Calculation**:
  - If sold at profit: `Selling Price = Carrying Value + Profit`
  - If sold at loss: `Selling Price = Carrying Value - Loss`

---

## Fixed Deposits

- **Increase** in Fixed Deposit = Money invested = **Outflow**.
- **Decrease** in Fixed Deposit = Money withdrawn = **Inflow**.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Additions**: Check if assets were bought on credit. If the question says "New equipment was bought on credit", it does **NOT** go in the Cash Flow Statement (no cash moved... yet). However, for Matric exams, usually assume cash unless stated otherwise or related to specific liabilities.
- **Replacements**: Sometimes the question asks for "Replacement of assets" vs "Expansion". Both are purchases (Outflows).

## Quick Check

1. If we sell a vehicle (CV R50k) at a profit of R10k, what amount goes into Investing Activities?
2. Calculate Purchases: Opening CV R100k, Closing CV R150k, Depreciation R20k. No disposals.
3. If Fixed Deposit goes from R50k to R80k, is it an inflow or outflow?
