# Internal Control & Ethics

## Internal Audit vs External Audit

| Feature | Internal Auditor | External Auditor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Appointed by** | Management / Directors | Shareholders |
| **Goal** | Improve internal controls & efficiency | Express opinion on Financial Statements |
| **Report to** | Board / Audit Committee | Shareholders |
| **Status** | Employee of the company | Independent firm |

## King Code of Governance

The "King Code" (King IV) sets the standard for ethical business in SA.
**Key Principles**:

1. **Transparency**: Openness. Don't hide information.
2. **Accountability**: Taking responsibility for actions.
3. **Fairness**: Treating all stakeholders (employees, community, shareholders) fairly.
4. **Social Responsibility**: Caring for the environment and community.

---

## Common Ethical Scenarios in Exams

- **Conflict of Interest**: A director awarding a contract to his wife's company.
- **Insider Trading**: A director selling shares because he knows bad news is coming (before the public knows). This is **Illegal**.
- **Window Dressing**: Manipulating accounts to make the profit look better (e.g., delaying recording expenses).

---

## Exam Tips

- **Solution**: When asked for a solution to an ethical problem, suggest:
  - Disciplinary hearing.
  - Declare conflict of interest (recuse oneself from voting).
  - Whistle-blowing policy.
