# Audit Reports

## Role of the Independent Auditor

- Appointed by **Shareholders** (at the AGM).
- Responsibility: To express an **opinion** on whether the financial statements are fairly presented.
- Duty: To protect the interests of shareholders (who are not involved in management).

## Types of Audit Opinions

### 1. Unqualified Opinion (The Best)

- **Meaning**: The financial statements fairly present the financial position of the company in all material respects.
- **Good News**: The auditors found no significant errors or fraud.
- *Tip for remembering: "Unqualified" sounds bad, but in auditing, it means "Without any complaints". It is a Clean Report.*

### 2. Qualified Opinion (Okay, but...)

- **Meaning**: The statements are mostly fair, **EXCEPT** for a specific issue (e.g., could not verify inventory at one branch).
- **The "BUT" clause**: Everything is fine, *except for*...

### 3. Adverse Opinion (Bad)

- **Meaning**: The statements do **NOT** fairly present the financial position.
- **Bad News**: There are material misstatements or fraud that affects the whole report.

### 4. Disclaimer of Opinion (The Worst)

- **Meaning**: The auditor could **NOT obtain enough evidence** to form an opinion.
- **Reason**: The company lost records, refused access, or is in chaos. The auditor basically says "I don't know what is going on here."

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**: You will be given an extract from a report. Look for keywords:
  - "Fairly present..." = Unqualified.
  - "Except for..." = Qualified.
  - "Do not fairly present..." = Adverse.
  - "We were unable to verify..." = Disclaimer.
