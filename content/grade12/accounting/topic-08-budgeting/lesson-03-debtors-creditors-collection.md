# Debtors Collection Schedule

Calculating when we will receive money from credit sales.

## The Pattern

"Debtors pay: 20% in month of sale, 50% in following month, 25% in month thereafter. 5% is bad debts."

**Example: Credit Sales**

- Jan: R100 000.
- Feb: R120 000.

**Collection in MARCH:**

- From Jan Sales: 25% (2 months later) = R25 000.
- From Feb Sales: 50% (1 month later) = R60 000.
- From Mar Sales: 20% (Current month) = [Need Mar Sales figure].

---

## Creditors Payment Schedule

Calculating when we pay suppliers.
Usually simpler: "Creditors are paid in full 60 days after purchase."

- Payment in March = Purchases from January.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Discount**: If debtors get a 5% discount for paying in the current month, remember to calculate on 95% of the amount.
  - `Sale × % collected × 95%`.
- **Cost of Sales**: Sometimes you are given Cost of Sales and Mark-up. You must calculate Credit Sales first before doing the schedule.

---

## Quick Check

1. If sales are R100k and terms are 30 days, when is the money received? (Next month).
2. If bad debts are 5%, do we put that money in the Cash Budget? (No, never received).
3. True or False: The Debtors Collection Schedule feeds into the Cash Budget "Receipts" section. (True).
