# Projected Income Statement

**Purpose**: To predict the future Profit (Success).
**Basis**: Accrual Basis (Expenses incurred, Income earned).

## Difference from Cash Budget

| Item | Cash Budget | Projected Income Statement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Credit Sales** | No (only when collected) | Yes (Total Sales) |
| **Credit Purchases** | No (only when paid) | Yes (Cost of Sales) |
| **Depreciation** | No | Yes |
| **Bad Debts** | No | Yes |
| **Loans** | Yes (Receipt/Repayment) | No (Only Interest) |
| **Asset Purchase** | Yes (Payment) | No (Asset capitalization) |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Comparison**: You are often asked to compare budgeted figures with actual figures.
- **Variance**: `Actual - Budget`.
- **Comment**: If Actual Rent > Budget Rent, did the landlord increase rent unexpectedly? Or did we rent more space?

## Quick Check

1. Which statement includes Depreciation? (Projected Income Statement).
2. Which statement includes the purchase of a vehicle for cash? (Cash Budget).
3. If we sell goods on credit in Jan, where is it recorded in Jan? (Projected Income Statement).
