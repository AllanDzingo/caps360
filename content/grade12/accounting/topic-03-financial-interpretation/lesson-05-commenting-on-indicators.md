# Structuring Your Comments

In exams, you are surprisingly often asked to "Comment on the liquidity..." or "Advise the directors...".
Marks are awarded for specific structure.

## The Recipe for Full Marks

1. **Quote the Indicators**: "The ratio increased from 1.5:1 in 2024 to 2:1 in 2025."
2. **State the Trend**: "This is an improvement / deterioration."
3. **Compare to Norm/Target**: "This is below the industry norm of 2:1."
4. **Explain the Consequence/Cause**: "This indicates the company is holding too much stock."
5. **Give Advice**: "The company should implement a Just-In-Time inventory system."

## Which Ratios to Use?

If the question asks about:

### 1. Liquidity (Can we pay debts?)

- Current Ratio
- Acid Test Ratio
- Debtors Collection vs Creditors Payment

### 2. Solvency (Are we bankrupt?)

- Solvency Ratio (Assets vs Liabilities)

### 3. Return (Are owners happy?)

- % Return on Equity (ROSHE) vs Interest on Fixed Deposits (Alternative).
- Earnings Per Share (EPS).
- Dividends Per Share (DPS).

### 4. Risk (Gearing)

- Debt-Equity Ratio.
- Return on Total Capital vs Interest Rate on Loans.

### 5. Dividend Policy

- Dividend Payout Rate = `(DPS ÷ EPS) × 100`.
- "The company retained 40% of profits for future growth."

---

## Exam Tips

- **Check the Mark Allocation**: If it's a 4 mark question, quote at least 2 indicators and give 2 comments.
- **Don't just say "It's good"**: Say *why* it is good. "It is good because it exceeds the inflation rate."
