# Profitability Indicators

These ratios measure how efficient the company is at generating profit from its sales.

## 1. Gross Profit on Sales (percentage)

- **Formula**: `(Gross Profit ÷ Sales) × 100`
- **Goal**: Maintain or increase the % from year to year.
- **Comment**: If it decreases, check if Cost of Sales increased (suppliers charged more) or if Selling Prices were lowered (discount sale).

## 2. Operating Profit on Sales (percentage)

- **Formula**: `(Operating Profit ÷ Sales) × 100`
- **Goal**: Indicates how well operating expenses are controlled.
- **Comment**: If Gross Profit % is good but Operating Profit % is bad, the problem is **Operating Expenses** (e.g., rent, salaries, electricity) are too high.

## 3. Net Profit on Sales (percentage)

- **Formula**: `(Net Profit after Tax ÷ Sales) × 100`
- **Goal**: The final "bottom line" return on every Rand of sales.

## 4. Operating Expenses on Sales (percentage)

- **Formula**: `(Operating Expenses ÷ Sales) × 100`
- **Goal**: Decrease this %.
- **Comment**: Compare with the inflation rate. If expenses increased by 15% but inflation is only 6%, expenses are not well controlled.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Compare**: Always compare the current year's % with the previous year's %.
- **Trend**: Identify if the trend is positive (improving) or negative (deteriorating).
- **Cause**: Suggest a reason. e.g., "Gross profit % decreased because the company ran a 'Buy 1 Get 1 Free' promotion."

## Quick Check

1. If Sales = R1 000 000 and Gross Profit = R400 000, what is the Gross Profit %?
2. If Operating Profit % dropped from 15% to 10%, which expenses should we look at?
3. True or False: A higher Operating Expenses % is better for the company.
