# Return and Gearing

This section is critical for shareholders deciding whether to invest.

## 1. % Return on Average Shareholders' Equity (ROSHE)

- **Formula**: `(Net Profit after Tax ÷ Average Shareholders' Equity) × 100`
- **Goal**: Must be higher than the return on alternative investments (e.g., Fixed Deposit interest rate ~7-9%).
- **Comment**: "The return of 15% is good because it exceeds the interest rate on alternative investments (8%)."

## 2. Debt-Equity Ratio (Gearing Ratio)

- **Formula**: `Non-Current Liabilities : Shareholders' Equity`
- **Norm**: Generally low risk if under 0.5:1. High risk if over 1:1.
- **Concept**:
  - **Low Gearing**: Mostly funded by own capital (Safe, but maybe missing growth opportunities).
  - **High Gearing**: Mostly funded by loans (Risky, high interest payments).

## 3. Return on Total Capital Employed (ROTCE)

- **Formula**: `(EBIT ÷ Average Capital Employed) × 100`
- **EBIT**: Earnings Before Interest and Tax (Profit before Tax + Interest Expense).
- **Capital Employed**: Shareholders Equity + Non-Current Liabilities.
- **Use**: Measures efficiency of ALL capital (loans + equity) combined.

## Positive vs Negative Gearing (Leverage)

- **Positive Gearing**: When the Return on Total Capital (ROTCE) > Interest Rate on Loans.
  - *Good*: We create extra wealth for shareholders using the bank's money.
- **Negative Gearing**: When ROTCE < Interest Rate on Loans.
  - *Bad*: The loan is costing more than the profit it generates. We are destroying value.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Average**: Always use `(Opening + Closing) / 2` for Equity and Capital Employed.
- **Risk**: Gearing = Risk. Higher debt = Higher financial risk.
- **Return**: ROSHE = Return.
