# Solvency Indicators

**Solvency** refers to the company's ability to pay its long-term debts. Can the company's total assets cover all its liabilities if it closed down today?

## 1. Solvency Ratio

- **Formula**: `Total Assets : Total Liabilities`
- **Norm**: Must be greater than 1:1. Ideally 2:1.
- **Comment**: If less than 1:1, the company is **Insolvent** (Bankrupt). Liabilities exceed Assets.

## 2. Net Asset Value (NAV) per share

- **Formula**: `(Ordinary Shareholders' Equity ÷ Number of Issued Shares) × 100` (answer in cents)
- **Use**: Compare NAV to the **Market Price** of the share (JSE price).
- **Comment**:
  - If Market Price > NAV: Investors are confident (Good goodwill/future prospects).
  - If Market Price < NAV: Investors are negative about the company.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Total Assets**: Non-Current Assets + Current Assets.
- **Total Liabilities**: Non-Current Liabilities + Current Liabilities.
- When commenting on NAV, always explicitly mention the **Market Price** given in the question. "Shareholders are satisfied because the market price (500c) is higher than the NAV (450c)."
