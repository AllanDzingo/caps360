# Liquidity Indicators

**Liquidity** refers to the company's ability to pay its short-term debts as they become due.

## 1. Current Ratio

- **Formula**: `Current Assets : Current Liabilities`
- **Norm**: Generally 2:1 is considered safe.
- **Comment**: If it's too low (e.g., 0.8:1), the company may struggle to pay debts. If too high (e.g., 5:1), the company is holding too much idle cash or stock.

## 2. Acid Test Ratio (Quick Ratio)

- **Formula**: `(Current Assets - Inventory) : Current Liabilities`
- **Norm**: 1:1.
- **Reason**: Inventory takes time to sell. This ratio tests if we can pay debts *immediately* using only cash and debtors.

## 3. Stock Holding Period (Inventory Turnover)

- **Formula**: `(Average Inventory ÷ Cost of Sales) × 365`
- **Goal**: Lower is usually better (selling stock fast).
- **Comment**: If too high (e.g., 180 days), stock is obsolete or "dead".

## 4. Debtors Collection Period

- **Formula**: `(Average Debtors ÷ Credit Sales) × 365`
- **Goal**: Usually 30 days.
- **Comment**: If 45 days, debtors are paying late. Cash flow will suffer.

## 5. Creditors Payment Period

- **Formula**: `(Average Creditors ÷ Credit Purchases) × 365`
- **Goal**: Usually 60-90 days.
- **Strategy**: Ideally, collect cash from debtors (30 days) *before* paying creditors (60 days).

---

## Exam Tips

- **Credit Purchases**: If not given, calculate as `Cost of Sales × % bought on credit` (often 100% or given in text).
- **Comment Structure**:
    1. Quote the ratio for this year and last year.
    2. Say if it improved or worsened.
    3. Compare to the norm/competitors.
    4. Give a specific solution (e.g., "Offer discounts to debtors for early payment").

## Quick Check

1. Why do we exclude Inventory in the Acid Test Ratio?
2. If Debtors Collection is 40 days and Creditors Payment is 30 days, is this good for cash flow?
3. Calculate Average Inventory if Opening is R10k and Closing is R20k.
