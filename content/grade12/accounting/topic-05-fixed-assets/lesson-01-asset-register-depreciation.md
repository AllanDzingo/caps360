# Fixed Assets

## The Asset Register

Every fixed asset (Vehicle, Equipment, Land) must be recorded in an Asset Register.
**Key info required:**

- Date of purchase.
- Cost price.
- Depreciation method and rate.
- Accumulated depreciation.

---

## Depreciation Methods

Depreciation is an expense (Operating Expense) that spreads the cost of an asset over its useful life.

### 1. Cost Price Method (Straight Line)

- **Formula**: `Cost Price × %`
- **Feature**: The depreciation amount is the *same* every year (unless bought/sold during the year).
- **Limit**: Accumulated depreciation cannot exceed (Cost - R1). The asset is kept at R1 scrap value.

### 2. Diminishing Balance Method (Reducing Balance)

- **Formula**: `(Cost - Accumulated Depreciation at start of year) × %`
- **Feature**: Depreciation is high in the first year and decreases every year as the asset gets older.
- **Carrying Value**: Cost - Accumulated Depreciation.

---

## Worked Example

**Asset**: Equipment bought for R100 000 on 1 March 2024.
**Rate**: 20% p.a.
**Year End**: 28 Feb 2025.

**Method A: Cost Price**
`R100 000 × 20% = R20 000`

**Method B: Diminishing Balance**
(Year 1 is same as Cost Price usually).
`R100 000 × 20% = R20 000`

**Year 2 Calculation (Diminishing Balance):**
Carrying Value = R100 000 - R20 000 = R80 000.
Depreciation = `R80 000 × 20% = R16 000`.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Timeline**: If an asset is bought during the year, ALWAYS apportion the depreciation.
  - `(Amount) × % × (Months/12)`
- **Land & Buildings**: Land does *not* depreciate. Buildings do.

---

## Quick Check

1. Which method results in higher depreciation in Year 1? (Usually same, unless bought mid-year).
2. What is the Carrying Value formula?
3. Can an asset have a Carrying Value of zero? (No, usually R1).
