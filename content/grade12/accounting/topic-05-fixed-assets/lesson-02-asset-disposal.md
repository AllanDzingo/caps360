# Asset Disposal

When we sell a Fixed Asset, we must remove it from our books and calculate the Profit or Loss on sale.

## The 5 Steps (Using the Asset Disposal Account)

Open an account called **Asset Disposal**.

1. **Remove Cost Price**:
    - Dr Asset Disposal (Cost Price).
    - Cr Equipment/Vehicle (Cost Price).
    *(The asset is now out of the books).*

2. **Update Depreciation**:
    - Calculate depreciation for the current year *up to the date of sale*.
    - Dr Depreciation (Expense).
    - Cr Accumulated Depreciation.

3. **Remove Accumulated Depreciation**:
    - Calculate the *total* accumulated depreciation (Opening balance + Step 2).
    - Dr Accumulated Depreciation.
    - Cr Asset Disposal.

4. **Record Selling Price**:
    - Cash sale: Dr Bank, Cr Asset Disposal.
    - Credit sale: Dr Debtors Control, Cr Asset Disposal.
    - Trade-in: Dr Creditors Control, Cr Asset Disposal.

5. **Determine Profit or Loss**:
    - Balance the Asset Disposal account.
    - **Dr Balance = Profit** (Income).
    - **Cr Balance = Loss** (Expense).

---

## Asset Disposal Note

The Fixed Assets Note must show:

- **Disposals at Carrying Value** (in brackets).
- Formula for Disposals CV: `Cost Price - Total Accumulated Depreciation at date of sale`.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Trade-ins**: If you trade in an old vehicle for a new one, remember the new vehicle is an *Addition* (Cost) and the old vehicle is a *Disposal* (Carrying Value).
- **Dates**: Watch the dates carefully! Most errors happen in Step 2 (calculating months).

---

## Quick Check

1. On which side of the Asset Disposal account do we put the Selling Price? (Credit).
2. If Carrying Value > Selling Price, did we make a Profit or Loss? (Loss).
3. If we sell on credit to a debtor, which account is debited? (Debtors Control).
