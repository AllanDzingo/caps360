# Introduction to Companies

## What is a Company?

A company is a **separate legal entity** that is owned by shareholders and managed by directors.  
This means the company exists independently from the people who own it.

In Accounting, companies are important because they:

- Can raise large amounts of capital
- Are taxed differently from sole traders
- Have strict reporting requirements (IFRS/GAAP)

---

## Key Characteristics of Companies

### 1. Legal Personality

A company can:

- Own assets
- Owe debts
- Enter into contracts  
in its **own name**, not the owner’s name.

### 2. Limited Liability

Shareholders are only responsible for the amount they invested (the cost of their shares).  
Their personal assets are protected if the company fails.

### 3. Continuity

A company continues to exist even if shareholders change or die. It has indefinite life.

### 4. Separation of Ownership and Control

- **Shareholders**: Own the company (provide capital).
- **Directors**: Manage the company (make decisions).

---

## Comparison with Sole Traders

| Aspect | Company | Sole Trader |
|-----|------|------|
| **Legal status** | Separate legal entity | Owner = business |
| **Liability** | Limited | Unlimited (Owner pays business debts) |
| **Capital** | Raised through issuing shares | Owner’s personal funds |
| **Continuity** | Yes (Indefinite) | No (Ends if owner dies/retires) |
| **Management** | Directors | Owner |
| **Tax** | Companies Tax (27%) | Personal Income Tax rates |

---

## Worked Example

Sipho buys 1 000 shares in "Sipho Tech (Pty) Ltd" at R5 each.

**Calculation of Share Capital:**

```
Share capital = Number of shares × Issue price
Share capital = 1 000 × R5
Share capital = R5 000
```

**Scenario:**
Sipho Tech goes bankrupt and owes the bank R100 000.  
How much does Sipho lose?

**Answer:**  
Sipho only loses his **R5 000** investment.  
The bank *cannot* take his personal car or house to pay the remaining debt. This is **Limited Liability**.

---

## Exam Tips (VERY IMPORTANT)

- Always mention **“separate legal entity”** when asked to define a company.
- When asking about the difference between a Public and Private company:
  - **Public (Ltd)**: Can list on JSE, offers shares to public, minimum 3 directors.
  - **Private ((Pty) Ltd)**: Cannot list on JSE, shares not for public, minimum 1 director.
- Do NOT confuse shareholders (owners) with directors (managers).

---

## Common Learner Mistakes

- Thinking shareholders run the day-to-day business (Directors do that).
- Confusing "Unlimited Liability" (Sole Trader/Partnership) with "Limited Liability" (Company).
- Forgetting that companies pay **Companies Tax** (currently 27%), not PAYE (employees pay PAYE).
