# Retained Income

## What is Retained Income?

Retained Income represents the **profits** that have been kept (retained) in the company over the years instead of being paid out as dividends.

- It is part of **Shareholders' Equity**.
- It is used to fund future growth or buy assets.

---

## The Retained Income Note (Format)

This is a mandatory note in the Financial Statements. You must know this layout by heart.

| **Retained Income** | **R** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Balance at beginning of year** | **X,XXX** |
| Net Profit after Tax | X,XXX |
| Repurchase of Shares (Premium portion) | (XXX) |
| Dividends on Ordinary Shares | (XXX) |
| -- *Paid (Interim)* | *(XXX)* |
| -- *Recommended (Final)* | *(XXX)* |
| **Balance at end of year** | **X,XXX** |

---

## Key Calculations

### 1. Net Profit After Tax

Always use the profit **after** the 27% tax has been deducted.
If given Profit *Before* Tax:

```
Profit After Tax = Profit Before Tax × (100% - 27%) / 100
OR
Profit After Tax = Profit Before Tax × 0.73
```

### 2. Repurchase of Shares (Premium)

As learned in Lesson 2:

- Only the **amount paid ABOVE the average share price** is subtracted here.
- Example: Average price R10. Buyback price R15.
- We subtract **R5 per share** bought back in this note.

### 3. Dividends

- Use the **Total Dividends** for the year (Interim + Final).
- This reduces the Retained Income.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Brackets**: Always show negatives (Bi-backs, Dividends) in brackets. Accounts marks are often lost here.
- **Missing Figure**: Often, examiners give the Opening and Closing balances and ask you to calculate the Net Profit or the Dividends. Work backwards!
- **Tax Rate**: Check the question paper for the specific tax rate (usually 27% or 28% depending on the year of the paper).

---

## Common Mistakes

- Subtracting the *entire* share buyback amount (instead of just the premium).
- Using "Profit Before Tax" instead of "Profit After Tax".
- Forgetting that "Dividends Paid" in the trial balance is usually just the Interim Dividend, and you must add the Final Dividend.
