# Share Capital

## Concepts of Share Capital

### 1. Authorized Share Capital

This is the **maximum** number of shares a company is allowed to issue according to its Memorandum of Incorporation (MOI).  
*Note: This number is listed in the notes but does not have a monetary value in the balance sheet.*

### 2. Issued Share Capital

This is the number of shares actually sold (issued) to shareholders.  
*Note: This is the figure used in calculations and the financial statements.*

---

## No-Par Value Shares

In South Africa, most companies use **No-Par Value** shares.

- Shares do not have a fixed "price tag".
- The price depends on what the market is willing to pay at the time of issue.
- Money received is credited to the **Ordinary Share Capital** account.

---

## Buying Back Shares (Share Buyback)

A company can buy back its own shares from existing shareholders.

- This reduces the number of issued shares.
- It usually increases the **Net Asset Value (NAV)** and **Earnings Per Share (EPS)** for remaining shareholders.
- **IMPORTANT**: When shares are bought back, we must split the payment into two parts:
    1. **Average Issue Price portion**: Reduces Share Capital.
    2. **Premium portion** (Amount paid *above* average): Reduces Retained Income.

---

## Worked Example: Share Capital Note

**Transaction Data:**

1. **Jan 1**: 100 000 shares in issue. Total value: R800 000.
2. **Mar 1**: Issued 50 000 new shares at R10 each.
3. **Jun 30**: Repurchased (Bought back) 10 000 shares at R12 each.

**Step 1: Calculate Average Share Price BEFORE Buyback**
We need the weighted average price *before* the buyback happens.

At Jan 1: 100 000 shares @ R800 000
New Issue: 50 000 shares @ (50 000 × R10) = R500 000

Total Shares = 100 000 + 50 000 = 150 000
Total Value = R800 000 + R500 000 = R1 300 000

**Average Price** = Total Value ÷ Total Number of Shares

```
Average Price = R1 300 000 ÷ 150 000
Average Price = R8,67 per share (rounded)
```

*(Let's use specific numbers for clean calculation in exams, usually decimals work out perfectly or are directed)*.

**Step 2: Record the Buyback of 10 000 shares at R12**

- **Share Capital Account**: 10 000 × R8,67 = R86 700 (Average Price)
- **Retained Income Account**: 10 000 × (R12 - R8,67) = R33 300 (Premium)

---

## Exam Tips

- **Always** calculate the specific **Average Share Price** at the date of the buyback. Do not use the initial price.
- New shares issued **increase** the average price if issued *above* the current average, and **decrease** it if issued *below*.
- The **Share Capital Note** is a very common exam question (10-15 marks). Memorize the format:
    1. Balance at beginning
    2. Shares issued during year
    3. Shares repurchased (at average price!)
    4. Balance at end

---

## Common Mistakes

- deducting the *full* buyback price from Share Capital (Wrong! Only the average price comes out of Share Capital).
- Taking the buyback premium from Share Capital instead of **Retained Income**.
- Confusing Authorized Shares (Maximum) with Issued Shares (Actual).
