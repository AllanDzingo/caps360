# Dividends

## What are Dividends?

Dividends are the portion of **Net Profit** that is distributed to the shareholders as a return on their investment.

## Types of Dividends

### 1. Interim Dividends

- Declared and paid halfway through the financial year.
- Based on the half-year profit.
- Recorded as: `Dividends on Ordinary Shares (Expense)` and `Bank (Asset decreases)`.

### 2. Final Dividends

- Declared at the end of the financial year.
- Recommended by directors, approved by shareholders.
- Often *declared* but not yet *paid* at year-end.
- Creates a liability: **Shareholders for Dividends**.

---

## The "Dividends on Ordinary Shares" Calculation

Total Dividends for the year = Interim Dividends + Final Dividends.

**IMPORTANT:**
We only pay dividends on shares that are **issued and outstanding** at the date of declaration.

- **Do NOT** pay dividends on shares that were bought back *before* the dividend date.
- **DO** pay dividends on new shares issued *before* the dividend date.

---

## Worked Example

**Timeline:**

- **28 Feb**: Financial Year End.
- **31 Aug (Interim)**: Company declares 15 cents per share. (100 000 shares in issue).
- **28 Feb (Final)**: Company declares 20 cents per share.
  - *Note: On 1 Jan, 20 000 new shares were issued.*
  - Total shares at year-end = 120 000.

**Calculations:**

**1. Interim Dividend (31 Aug)**
Shares = 100 000
Dividend = 15c (R0.15)

```
100 000 × 0.15 = R15 000
```

*Paid immediately.*

**2. Final Dividend (28 Feb)**
Shares = 120 000 (Includes new issue)
Dividend = 20c (R0.20)

```
120 000 × 0.20 = R24 000
```

*Recorded as Shareholders for Dividends (Liability).*

**Total Dividends for Retained Income Note:**
R15 000 + R24 000 = **R39 000**

---

## Exam Tips

- **Dates matter!** Draw a timeline. Check if the shares were issued/bought back *before* or *after* the dividend date.
- If the question says "Dividends Paid" in the Cash Flow Statement, this refers to CASH outflow (Last year's final + This year's interim).
- If the question asks for "Dividends Provided for" or "Dividends Declared", this is the Total Dividends (Interim + Final) for the current year.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Calculating the final dividend on the *old* number of shares (forgetting new issues).
- Confusing "Shareholders for Dividends" (Balance Sheet Liability) with "Dividends on Ordinary Shares" (Retained Income Expense).
- Forgetting to convert "cents" to "Rands" (e.g., calculating 50c as 50 instead of 0.50).
