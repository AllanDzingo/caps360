# Statement of Financial Position

Also known as the **Balance Sheet**.
**Goal:** Show the financial position (Assets = Equity + Liabilities) at a specific date.

## Important Adjustments

### 1. Fixed Deposit (Financial Assets)

- **Maturing portion**: If any part of the Fixed Deposit matures within the next 12 months, move that portion to **Cash and Cash Equivalents**. The rest stays in Non-Current.

### 2. Loan (Non-Current Liability)

- **Current Portion**: The capital repayment to be made in the next 12 months must be moved to **Current Liabilities**.
- **Capitalized Interest**: Ensure the closing balance includes capitalized interest.

### 3. SARS: Income Tax

- If we owe SARS (Debit balance in Tax account): It is an Asset (Trade & Other Receivables).
- If SARS owes us (Credit balance in Tax account): It is a Liability (Trade & Other Payables).
- *Correction*: **Debit Balance = We paid too much = ASSET.** **Credit Balance = We owe SARS = LIABILITY.**
- Usually shown separately on the face of the Balance Sheet or inside Trade Payables/Receivables notes.

### 4. Shareholders for Dividends

- This is the **Final Dividend** calculated in Lesson 3. It is always a Current Liability.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Balancing**: If your Balance Sheet doesn't balance, don't panic. You earn marks for every correct line item. Do not waste 10 minutes looking for a R100 difference.
- **Ordinary Share Capital & Retained Income**: These figures come directly from the notes you prepared in previous questions.
- **Bank Overdraft**: If the bank account is in overdraft, it is a Current Liability. If positive, it is a Current Asset.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting to move the **Current Portion of Loan** to Current Liabilities.
- Incorrectly classifying the SARS balance (Asset vs Liability).
- Leaving out valid notes (Inventory, Trade Receivables) calculations.
