# Statement of Changes in Equity

This statement explains how the owners' interest (Equity) has changed during the year.
It combines the **Share Capital** and **Retained Income** movements into one table.

## Format (The "Matrix" View)

| Details | Share Capital | Retained Income | Total Equity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Balance at beginning of year** | **X,XXX** | **X,XXX** | **X,XXX** |
| Net Profit after Tax | - | X,XXX | X,XXX |
| Shares Issued | X,XXX | - | X,XXX |
| Shares Repurchased (Buyback) | (XXX) | (XXX) | (XXX) |
| Dividends | - | (XXX) | (XXX) |
| **Balance at end of year** | **X,XXX** | **X,XXX** | **X,XXX** |

---

## Understanding the Movements

### 1. Shares Issued

- Increases **Share Capital**.
- Increases **Total Equity**.
- No effect on Retained Income.

### 2. Net Profit

- Increases **Retained Income**.
- Increases **Total Equity**.

### 3. Share Buyback

- Decreases **Share Capital** (by Average Price).
- Decreases **Retained Income** (by Premium paid above average).
- Decreases **Total Equity** (by total cash paid).

### 4. Dividends

- Decreases **Retained Income**.
- Decreases **Total Equity**.

---

## Worked Example

**Data:**

- Opening Equity: Share Capital R100k, Retained Income R50k.
- Profit: R80k.
- Dividend: R20k.
- Buyback: R10k total (R6k capital, R4k premium).

**Filling the Table:**

| Details | Share Capital | Retained Income | Total |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Opening Balance** | **100 000** | **50 000** | **150 000** |
| Net Profit | - | 80 000 | 80 000 |
| Buyback | (6 000) | (4 000) | (10 000) |
| Dividends | - | (20 000) | (20 000) |
| **Closing Balance** | **94 000** | **106 000** | **200 000** |

---

## Exam Tips

- If you are asked to prepare the **Statement of Changes in Equity**, draw this column format.
- If you are asked for **Notes to the Balance Sheet**, do the separate Share Capital and Retained Income notes (Lesson 2 & 4).
- **Check numbers**: Opening + Movements must = Closing. Vertical totals must also match horizontal totals.

## Quick Check

1. Does a share issue affect Retained Income?
2. Which column does Net Profit affect?
3. If Total Equity increases by R50 000 and Share Capital increased by R20 000, and there were no dividends/buybacks, what was the Net Profit?
