# Production Cost Statement

This statement calculates the total cost of manufacturing finished goods.

## The Format (Recipe)

| Details | R |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Direct Material Cost** | X,XXX |
| **Direct Labour Cost** | X,XXX |
| **Prime Cost** | **(A)** |
| Factory Overhead Cost | X,XXX |
| **Total Manufacturing Cost** | **(B)** |
| Work-in-Process (Opening) | + XX |
| Work-in-Process (Closing) | (XX) |
| **Cost of Production of Finished Goods** | **(C)** |

---

## Key Calculations

### 1. Direct Material Cost

- Opening Stock (Raw Material)
- (+) Purchases
- (+) Carriage on Purchases
- (-) Closing Stock (Raw Material)
- **= Direct Material Cost**

### 2. Direct Labour Cost

- Factory wages (Workers who make the product).
- (+) Contributions (UIF, Pension) paid by employer.
- **Do not include**: Factory Supervisor salary (Indirect Labour -> Overhead).

### 3. Factory Overhead Cost

- Everything else in the factory that is not Direct Material or Direct Labour.
- Indirect Materials (Cleaning agents, glue).
- Indirect Labour (Supervisor, Cleaners).
- Factory Rent, Electricity, Water.
- Depreciation on Factory Machinery.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Prime Cost**: Direct Material + Direct Labour.
- **Administration Cost**: This goes to the Income Statement, NOT the Production Cost Statement.
- **Selling & Distribution**: This goes to the Income Statement, NOT the Production Cost Statement.
