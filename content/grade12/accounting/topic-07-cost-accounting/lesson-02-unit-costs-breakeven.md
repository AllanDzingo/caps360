# Unit Costs and Break-even

## Variable vs Fixed Costs

- **Variable Costs**: Change in total as production changes. (e.g., Raw Materials, Direct Labour, Selling Commission).
  - *Variable Cost per unit stays constant.*
- **Fixed Costs**: Stay the same in total regardless of production. (e.g., Factory Rent, Insurance, Admin Salaries).
  - *Fixed Cost per unit decreases as production increases.*

## Break-even Point (BEP)

The point where the business makes NO profit and NO loss. Total Income = Total Costs.

### Formula

```
BEP (Units) = Total Fixed Costs ÷ (Selling Price per unit - Variable Cost per unit)
```

The bottom part `(SP - VC)` is called the **Contribution Margin**.

---

## Commenting on BEP

- **If Production > BEP**: The business is making a profit. (Good).
- **If Production < BEP**: The business is making a loss. (Bad).
- **Safety Margin**: `Current Production - BEP`. How much can sales drop before we start making a loss?

---

## Exam Tips

- **Strategy**: To improve profit:
    1. Increase Selling Price (may lose customers).
    2. Decrease Variable Costs (find cheaper suppliers).
    3. Increase Production (spreads fixed costs).

## Quick Check

1. Give one example of a Fixed Cost.
2. If Selling Price is R10, Variable Cost is R6, Total Fixed Cost is R1000. Calculate BEP. (R1000 / 4 = 250 units).
3. If we produce 300 units (from Q2), are we making a profit? (Yes, because 300 > 250).
