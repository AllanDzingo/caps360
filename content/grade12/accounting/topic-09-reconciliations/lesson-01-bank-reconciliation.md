# Bank Reconciliation

**Purpose**: To check if our books (Bank Account) match the bank's books (Bank Statement).

## The Process

1. **Compare**: Tick off items that appear in both the Journals (CPJ/CRJ) and the Bank Statement.
2. **Update Journals**:
    - Items in Bank Statement but *not* in Journals must be added to Journals.
    - Examples: Bank Charges, Direct Deposits, Stop Orders, Dishonoured Cheques (R/D).
3. **Prepare Bank Reconciliation Statement**:
    - Items in Journals but *not* in Bank Statement (Outstanding items).
    - Errors made by the Bank.

## Format of Bank Reconciliation Statement

| Details | Debit | Credit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Balance per Bank Statement** | XX | XX |
| Outstanding Deposit | | XX |
| Outstanding EFTs / Cheques | XX | |
| Bank Error (Correction) | XX | XX |
| **Balance per Bank Account** (Final calculated balance) | XX | XX |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Favourable vs Unfavourable**:
  - **Bank Statement**: Credit is Favourable (+) (Bank owes you). Debit is Unfavourable (-) (Overdraft).
  - **Bank Account (Ledger)**: Debit is Favourable (+). Credit is Unfavourable (-).
- **Post-dated Cheques received**: These go to the "Cheques Accepted" register, NOT the CRJ, until the date arrives.

## Quick Check

1. If the Bank Statement has a Credit Balance, is this good or bad for us? (Good).
2. Where do "Service Fees" go? (CPJ).
3. Where does an "Outstanding Deposit" go? (Bank Recon Statement).
