# FIFO (First-In-First-Out)

**Concept**: We assume that the first units we bought are the first ones we sold.
Therefore, the units left on the shelf (Closing Stock) are the **newest/most recently bought** units.

## Calculating Closing Stock Value

1. Start with the **Last** units bought (most recent month).
2. Count backwards until you reach the number of units on hand.
3. Sum their values.

## Worked Example

- Units on hand: **15 units**.
- Purchases:
  - Jan: 10 units @ R5 = R50
  - Feb: 10 units @ R6 = R60
  - Mar: 10 units @ R8 = R80

**Calculation (FIFO)**:
We need 15 units.

- Take all 10 from March: 10 @ R8 = **R80**.
- Need 5 more. Take from Feb: 5 @ R6 = **R30**.
- **Total Value**: R80 + R30 = **R110**.

## Impact of Inflation

- Under FIFO, closing stock is valued at the **recent (higher) prices**.
- This makes Gross Profit **higher** (Cost of Sales is lower because we "sold" the cheap old stock).

---

## Exam Tips

- **Carriage**: Always add carriage/delivery costs to the unit price before calculating.
- **Returns**: If units were returned to supplier, subtract them from the specific batch they came from.

2. Does FIFO usually result in higher or lower profit during inflation?
