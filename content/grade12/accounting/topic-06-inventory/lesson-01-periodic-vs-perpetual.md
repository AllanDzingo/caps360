# Inventory Systems

## 1. Perpetual Inventory System (Continuous)

- **Concept**: Records every sale and cost of sale *immediately* at the till point.
- **Accounts Used**: `Trading Stock` and `Cost of Sales`.
- **Used by**: Supermarkets (Checkers, PnP), clothing stores (scanning barcodes).
- **Pros**: Can check stock levels anytime on computer. Good control.
- **Cons**: Expensive software needed.

## 2. Periodic Inventory System (Count-based)

- **Concept**: Records cost of sales only at the *end* of the period after a physical count.
- **Accounts Used**: `Purchases` (Expense), `Opening Stock`, `Closing Stock`.
- **Logic**: No "Trading Stock" account during the year. We buy into "Purchases".
- **Calculation**: Cost of Sales = Opening Stock + Purchases - Closing Stock.
- **Used by**: Small shops, spaza shops, coal yards (hard to scan).
- **Pros**: Cheap, simple.
- **Cons**: No real-time stock control. Theft is hard to detect until year-end.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Identification**: Look at the Trial Balance. if you see "Purchases", it's Periodic. If you see "Cost of Sales", it's Perpetual.
- **Carriage on Purchases**:
  - Perpetual: Added to Trading Stock account.
  - Periodic: Separate expense account.
