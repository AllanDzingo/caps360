# Ethics vs Professionalism

These two concepts are often confused, but they are different.

## 1. Professionalism

- **Definition**: Meeting the standards of a profession or job. Performing a task with competence and skill.
- **Focus**: The *Job/Action*.
- **Examples**: Arriving on time, dressing appropriately, treating customers with respect, using polite language.

## 2. Ethics

- **Definition**: Moral principles that govern a person's behaviour or the conducting of an activity. Knowing Right vs Wrong.
- **Focus**: The *Moral Choice*.
- **Examples**: Not stealing, adhering to laws, paying fair wages, not polluting.

---

## Comparison Table

| Aspect | Professionalism | Ethics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Origin** | Business rules / Code of Conduct | Moral values / Society |
| **Includes** | Appearance, punctuality, competence | Honesty, integrity, fairness |
| **Violation** | Usually disciplinary hearing | Crime or reputational damage |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**:
  - "John is late for meetings" -> Unprofessional.
  - "John steals money" -> Unethical.
  - "John is rude to clients" -> Unprofessional.
  - "John dumps toxic waste in the river" -> Unethical.

## Quick Check

1. Define Professionalism in one sentence.
2. Is lying to a customer Unprofessional or Unethical? (Both, but primarily Unethical as it's dishonest).
3. Is wearing slops (flip-flops) to a formal meeting Unprofessional or Unethical? (Unprofessional).
