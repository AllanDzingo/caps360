# King Code of Governance

The King Code provides guidelines for ethical and effective leadership.

## The Principles (T-A-R)

### 1. Transparency (Openness)

- Decisions and actions must be clear to all stakeholders.
- Regular, honest reporting.
- **Example**: Publishing full financial statements, not hiding debts.

### 2. Accountability (Answering for actions)

- Directors are answerable for their decisions.
- If things go wrong, they must take the blame and fix it.
- **Example**: A CEO resigning after a corruption scandal.

### 3. Responsibility (Role)

- Directors must act with skill and care.
- They have a duty to act in the best interest of the company (not themselves).
- Includes **Social Responsibility** (Caring for community/environment).

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**: "The directors refused to show the financial statements to the shareholders."
  - *Principle Violated*: Transparency.
- **Scenario**: "The factory dumped chemical waste in the river to save money."
  - *Principle Violated*: Responsibility (Social/Environmental).

## Quick Check

1. What does the "T" in T-A-R stand for?
2. If a director makes a bad decision and blames his assistant, which principle is he violating? (Accountability).
3. Does the King Code apply only to big companies? (No, it applies to all entities, including NGOs and schools).
