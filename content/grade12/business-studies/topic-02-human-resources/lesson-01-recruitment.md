# Recruitment

## What is Recruitment?

The process of finding and attracting suitable candidates for a job vacancy.

## Step 1: Job Analysis

Before recruiting, HR must define the job:

1. **Job Description**: Describes the **duties** and responsibilities (What you do).
    - e.g., "Answer phones," "Type reports."
2. **Job Specification**: Describes the **qualifications** and skills required (Who you are).
    - e.g., "Matric," "3 years experience," "Friendly personality."

## Step 2: Method of Recruitment

### 1. Internal Recruitment

Looking inside the business.

- **Methods**: Notice board, internal email, promotion, transfer.
- **Pros**: Cheaper, faster, boosts morale (promotion), candidate is known.
- **Cons**: No new ideas, jealousy among staff, creates another vacancy to fill.

### 2. External Recruitment

Looking outside the business.

- **Methods**: LinkedIn, Newspapers, Recruitment Agencies, Schools/Universities.
- **Pros**: New ideas/blood, larger pool of candidates.
- **Cons**: Expensive, risk of bad hire, induction takes longer.

---

## Exam Tips

- Distinguish clearly between Job *Description* (Tasks) and Job *Specification* (Person).
- Scenarios often ask: "Advise the business on whether to use internal or external recruitment." Look for clues like "The business needs fresh ideas" (External) or "The business has limited funds" (Internal).

## Quick Check

1. "Must have a driver's license" - Is this a Job Description or Specification?
2. Name ONE advantage of Internal Recruitment.
3. Give an example of an External Recruitment method.
