# Fringe Benefits

Rewards given to employees *in addition* to their salary/wage.

## Types of Benefits

### 1. Compulsory Benefits (Required by Law)

- **UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund)**: 1% paid by employee, 1% by employer.
- **Paid Leave**: Annual, Sick, Family Responsibility (BCEA requirements).
- **COIDA**: Employer pays levy for injuries.

### 2. Non-Compulsory Benefits (Optional)

- Medical Aid contribution.
- Pension / Provident Fund.
- Car Allowance / Travel Allowance.
- Housing Allowance.
- Performance Bonuses.
- Staff Loans / Discounts.

---

## Unemployment Insurance Fund (UIF)

- **Purpose**: Provides short-term relief to workers when they become unemployed or are unable to work (Maternity, Illness).
- **Claiming**: Workers claim from the Department of Labour / UIF Fund.

---

## Impact of Fringe Benefits

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Attracts better quality staff. | Costly for the business. |
| Motivates employees (Loyalty). | Admin intensive to manage. |
| Tax deductions for some benefits. | Employees may strike if benefits are reduced. |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Pie**: Think of "Cost to Company" packages. Salary is the base, Benefits are the toppings.
- If a business wants to cut costs, they usually cut *Non-Compulsory* benefits first, but this demotivates staff.
- **Maternity**: Remember, employers do *not* have to pay salary during maternity leave, but the employee can claim from UIF.
