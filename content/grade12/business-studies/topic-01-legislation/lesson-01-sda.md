# Skills Development Act (SDA)

## Purpose of the SDA

The SDA was introduced to address the skills shortage in South Africa and redress past inequalities in education.

- To develop the skills of the South African workforce.
- To encourage employers to use the workplace as a learning environment.
- To improve the employment prospects of previously disadvantaged persons.

## Key Provisions

### 1. Skills Development Levy (SDL)

- Employers with a payroll over R500 000 per year must pay **1%** of their payroll as a levy to SARS.
- This money funds the SETAs (Sector Education and Training Authorities).

### 2. SETAs

- Each industry (e.g., Banking, Agriculture) has a SETA.
- They develop sector skills plans and approve learnerships.

---

## Impact on Business

| **Positives (Advantages)** | **Negatives (Disadvantages)** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Improves productivity of employees due to better skills. | Costly to pay the 1% levy (extra tax). |
| Businesses can claim back grants if they train staff. | Administrative burden to complete reports and plans. |
| Increases global competitiveness. | Employees may leave after training for better jobs. |
| Promotes Black Economic Empowerment (BEE) points. | |

---

## Compliance vs Non-Compliance

**Compliance Actions:**

- Register with SARS for SDL.
- Submit a Workplace Skills Plan (WSP) and Annual Training Report (ATR).
- Pay the 1% levy.

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**

- Fines from the Department of Labour.
- Cannot claim grants from the SETA.
- Loss of BEE points.

## Exam Tips (PESTLE Context)

- The SDA falls under the **Legal** and **Social** environments.
- **Scenario Question:** "ABC Traders refuses to pay the levy because they have valid training internally."
  - *Answer:* They are non-compliant. The levy is mandatory if payroll > R500k. They can claim money back *after* paying, but they cannot refuse to pay.
