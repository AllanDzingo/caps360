# Labour Relations Act (LRA)

## Purpose of the LRA

The LRA governs the relationship between **Employers**, **Employees**, and **Trade Unions**.

- To enforce the Constitutional right to fair labour practices.
- To promote economic development and social justice.
- To facilitate collective bargaining and dispute resolution.

## Key Rights

- **Employees**: Right to join a trade union, right to strike, right to fair labour practices.
- **Employers**: Right to form employers' organisations, right to lockout (recourse to lockout), right to dismiss (fairly).

---

## Impact on Business

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Provides a clear framework for resolving disputes (less chaos). | Strikes can lead to loss of production. |
| Protects employers/employees from unfair actions. | Employers cannot dismiss workers easily (strict procedures). |
| Promotes communication via Workplace Forums. | Time-consuming to attend CCMA hearings. |

---

## Discriminatory Actions (Non-Compliance)

- Preventing an employee from joining a union.
- Dismissing a woman because she is pregnant.
- Forcing an employee to do forced labour.

## Dispute Resolution Mechanisms

1. **Bargaining Councils**: formed by unions and employer associations in a specific sector.
2. **CCMA (Commission for Conciliation, Mediation and Arbitration)**:
    - Independent body.
    - Resolves disputes via **Conciliation** (Talking) or **Arbitration** (Judge decides).
3. **Labour Court**: For serious cases (e.g., unfair dismissal) if CCMA fails.

---

## Exam Tips

- Remember the difference between a **Strike** (Employees stop work) and a **Lockout** (Employers prevent access to workplace).
- **Go-slow**: Employees work but very slowly. This IS a form of industrial action regulated by the LRA.
- **Protected vs Unprotected Strike**:
  - *Protected*: Followed procedures (LRA). Cannot be fired.
  - *Unprotected*: Did not follow procedures. Can be fired after hearing.
