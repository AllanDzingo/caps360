# BBBEE Act

## Purpose

- To distribute wealth across a broader spectrum of previously disadvantaged South Africans (not just a few wealthy individuals).
- To target inequality in Ownership, Management, and Skills.

## The Pillars (The Scorecard)

Most recent codes use these 5 pillars:

### 1. Ownership (Priority Element)

- % of shares owned by black people.

### 2. Management Control

- % of black directors and top management.

### 3. Skills Development (Priority Element)

- Spending on training for black employees.

### 4. Enterprise and Supplier Development (Priority Element)

- Preferential Procurement: Buying from BEE compliant suppliers.
- Enterprise Development: Helping small black businesses grow.

### 5. Socio-Economic Development (SED)

- CSI projects (Investments in community, education, etc.).

---

## Impact

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Allows access to government tenders (Must be compliant). | Fronting (Fake BEE status) is a problem. |
| Improves standard of living for majority. | Compliance can be expensive (Consultants). |
| Encourages skills development. | Political interference/corruption. |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Fronting**: When a company pretends to be BEE compliant (e.g., appointing a black director who has no power) to get tenders. This is **Illegal** (Fraud).
- **Levels**: Level 1 Contributor = Best (135% procurement recognition). Level 8 = Worst compliant. Non-compliant = 0%.
