# Consumer Protection Act (CPA)

## Purpose

- To protect the rights of consumers.
- To prevent unfair business practices.

## The 8 Fundamental Rights

1. **Right to Equality**: No discrimination in pricing or access.
2. **Right to Privacy**: Right to refuse unwanted marketing (SMS/Email).
3. **Right to Choose**: Right to choose suppliers, right to cool-off period (5 days for direct marketing).
4. **Right to Disclosure and Information**: Contracts in plain language, price must be displayed.
5. **Right to Fair and Honest Dealing**: No misleading advertising or pyramid schemes.
6. **Right to Fair Terms and Conditions**: No unfair "fine print".
7. **Right to Quality and Safety**: Goods must be safe and working. (Implied warranty of 6 months).
    - *If it breaks in 6 months, consumer can choose: Repair, Replace, or Refund.*
8. **Right to Accountability by Suppliers**: Suppliers are liable for damage caused by unsafe goods.

---

## Impact

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Builds customer loyalty (trust). | Costs money to replace/refund goods. |
| Businesses improve quality standards. | Staff need training on the Act. |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Implied Warranty**: Remind learners about the 6-month rule. It overrides store policies like "No Refunds".
- **Cooling-off Period**: Only applies if YOU approached the customer (Direct Marketing). Does not apply if they walked into your store.
