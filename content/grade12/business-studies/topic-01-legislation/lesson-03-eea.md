# Employment Equity Act (EEA)

## Purpose of the EEA

- To eliminate unfair discrimination in the workplace.
- To promote **equal opportunity** and fair treatment.
- To implement **Affirmative Action** measures to redress disadvantages experienced by designated groups.

## Designated Groups (PDI)

- Black people (African, Coloured, Indian).
- Women.
- People with disabilities.

---

## Impact on Business

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Promotes diversity in the workplace. | Expensive to implement (training/admin). |
| Encourages consultation between employer/employees. | Impact on productivity if suitable candidates aren't found. |
| Avoids heavy fines for non-compliance. | Some skilled workers may leave (Brain Drain) if they feel excluded. |

---

## Compliance Requirements

1. **Conduct an Analysis**: Assess the demographic profile of staff.
2. **Employment Equity Plan**: Prepare and implement a plan to reach targets.
3. **Report to Department of Labour**: Submit reports regularly.
4. **Display Summary**: Must display a summary of the Act in the workplace.

## Penalties

- Heavy fines from the Department of Labour.
- Prosecution in the Labour Court.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Discrimination**: The EEA allows "Fair" discrimination (Affirmative Action) but prohibits "Unfair" discrimination (Race, Gender, Religion etc.).
- Do not confuse with **B-BBEE**.
  - *EEA*: Focuses on *Employment* (Staff).
  - *B-BBEE*: Focuses on *Distribution of Wealth* (Ownership, Management, Suppliers, CSI).

---

## Quick Check

1. Who are the "Designated Groups" under the EEA?
2. Give one example of "Unfair Discrimination".
3. True or False: The EEA forces businesses to fire white males. (False - it encourages hiring/promoting designated groups, not firing).
