# Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA)

## Purpose of the BCEA

- To establish and enforce basic conditions of employment.
- To prevent exploitation of workers.
- To regulate working hours, leave, and termination.

## Key Provisions

### 1. Working Hours

- **Ordinary Hours**: Max 45 hours per week. (9 hours/day for 5-day week, 8 hours/day for 6-day week).
- **Overtime**: Max 10 hours per week (Or 3 hours per day). Paid at **1.5x** normal rate.
- **Sundays/Public Holidays**: Paid at **2x** normal rate.

### 2. Leave

- **Annual Leave**: 21 consecutive days per year.
- **Sick Leave**: 30 days in a 3-year cycle (paid).
- **Maternity Leave**: 4 consecutive months (unpaid by employer, claimed from UIF).
- **Family Responsibility Leave**: 3 days per year (paid) for death of immediate family or sickness of child.

### 3. Termination

- **Notice Period**:
  - Employed < 6 months: 1 week notice.
  - Employed 6-12 months: 2 weeks notice.
  - Employed > 1 year: 4 weeks notice.

---

## Impact on Business

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Creates fair/humane working conditions. | Increases labour costs (overtime/leave). |
| Reduces uncertainty (clear rules). | Employers cannot force workers to work excessive hours to meet deadlines. |
| Prevents exploitation. | Administrative burden (keeping records). |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Exclusions**: The BCEA does NOT apply to:
  - Members of the SANDF (Army).
  - Intelligence Agency (Spies).
  - Unpaid volunteers.
  - Independent contractors.
- **Meal Intervals**: Workers must have a 60 min break after 5 hours of work.
