# National Credit Act (NCA)

## Purpose

- To promote a fair and non-discriminatory credit market.
- To protect consumers from **Reckless Lending**.
- To ensure consumers understand what they are signing (Plain Language).

## Key Provisions

- **Affordability Assessment**: Credit providers (Banks, Stores) MUST check if the consumer can afford the repayment before granting credit.
- **Interest Caps**: Limits the maximum interest rate charged.
- **Language**: Contracts must be simple and understandable.
- **Reasons**: Consumers have a right to know why credit was refused.

## Reckless Lending

Credit is reckless if:

1. Provider did not assess affordability.
2. Consumer did not understand the risks.
3. Granting the credit made the consumer over-indebted.
*Consequence*: The debt can be written off by the court.

---

## Impact on Business

| **Positives** | **Negatives** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Lowers bad debts (customers can afford to pay). | Sales may decrease (less credit granted). |
| Protects the economy from recession (credit bubbles). | Admin burden (Checks take time). |

---

## Exam Tips

- **NCA does NOT apply** to handling credit between businesses (B2B) if the turnover is large. It is mainly for protecting *individuals*.
- **Debt Counselling**: If a consumer is over-indebted, they can apply for Debt Review. The debt counsellor restructures the debt.
