# CSR vs CSI

## 1. Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

- **Concept**: The *strategy* or *attitude* of the business to take responsibility for its impact on society and the environment.
- **Internal**: Treating employees well (Safety, Fair pay).
- **External**: Caring for the community and environment.
- **Key**: It is the "Umbrella" term.

## 2. Corporate Social Investment (CSI)

- **Concept**: The actual *money* or *resources* spent on specific projects.
- **Action**: It is the practical implementation of CSR.
- **Examples**: Building a school, sponsoring a sports team, donating food.

---

## Comparison Table

| Aspect | CSR | CSI |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | The *Obligation* / Policy | The *Action* / Project |
| **Goal** | Sustainable business practice | Uplifting the community |
| **Link** | Linked to core business | Often charitable (not core business) |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**:
  - "Woolworths has a policy to use less plastic." -> **CSR** (Internal policy).
  - "Woolworths donated R1 million to Education." -> **CSI** (Money spent).
