# Comparing Forms of Ownership

## 1. Sole Trader

- **Owners**: 1.
- **Name**: Ends in nothing.
- **Liability**: Unlimited.
- **Pros**: Easy to start, keep all profit.
- **Cons**: High risk, hard to raise capital.

## 2. Partnership

- **Owners**: 2 or more.
- **Agreement**: Partnership Agreement (Written or Verbal).
- **Liability**: Unlimited (Jointly and Severally liable).
- **Pros**: Shared capital and skills.
- **Cons**: Conflict, unlimited liability.

## 3. Private Company (Pty) Ltd

- **Owners**: 1 or more Shareholders.
- **Name**: Ends in "(Pty) Ltd".
- **Liability**: Limited.
- **Pros**: Continuity, Limited Liability.
- **Cons**: More regulation (CIPC registration), Audits may be required.
- **Restriction**: Cannot sell shares to the public.

## 4. Public Company (Ltd)

- **Owners**: Shareholders (Public).
- **Name**: Ends in "Ltd".
- **Liability**: Limited.
- **Pros**: Can raise huge capital on JSE.
- **Cons**: Strict regulation, expensive to set up, no privacy of financials.

---

## Other Forms

- **NPC (Non-Profit Company)**: Aim is not profit but social benefit. Ends in "NPC".
- **SOC (State-Owned Company)**: Owned by Government. e.g., Eskom. Ends in "SOC Ltd".

---

## Exam Tips

- **Close Corporations (CC)**: You cannot *start* a new CC anymore (since 2008). Existing ones can stay. treated like Pty Ltd.
- **Jointly and Severally**: If your partner runs away with the money, YOU surely must pay all the debts.

## Quick Check

1. What does "(Pty) Ltd" stand for? (Proprietary Limited).
2. Which company type lists shares on the JSE? (Public Company - Ltd).
3. If a Sole Trader dies, what happens to the business? (It ceases to exist / closes).
