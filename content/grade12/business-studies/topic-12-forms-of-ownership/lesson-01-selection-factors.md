# Factors in Choosing a Form of Ownership

When starting a business, you must choose the right structure (Sole Trader, Partnership, Close Corporation, Pty Ltd, Public Company).

## The Factors

### 1. Liability (Risk)

- **Unlimited Liability**: The owner is personally responsible for the debts of the business. If the business fails, they can lose their house/car. (Sole Trader, Partnership).
- **Limited Liability**: The owner only loses the money they invested. Personal assets are safe. (Companies).

### 2. Capital (Money)

- How much capital is needed?
- **Small**: Sole Trader.
- **Huge**: Public Company (can sell shares to the public on JSE).

### 3. Management

- Who will run the business?
- **Sole Trader**: Quick decisions, but limited skills.
- **Company**: Managed by Directors. Slower decisions but expert skills.

### 4. Continuity

- What happens if the owner dies/retires?
- **No Continuity**: Business closes. (Sole Trader).
- **Continuity**: Business continues existing. (Companies).

### 5. Taxation

- **Personal Income Tax**: Sole Trader / Partners (Progressive tax tables).
- **Companies Tax**: Fixed rate (currently 27%) on profits.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**: "John wants to protect his personal assets." -> Advise him to choose a **Company** (Pty Ltd) for **Limited Liability**.
- **Scenario**: "Needs to raise billions to build a mine." -> **Public Company** (JSE).
