# Leadership Styles

A leader must choose the right style for the situation.

## 1. Democratic (Participative)

- **Style**: Involves employees in decision making. "What do you think?"
- **When to use**: Skilled, experienced employees. Complex problems.
- **Pros**: High morale, new ideas.
- **Cons**: Time consuming.

## 2. Autocratic (Bossy)

- **Style**: Tells employees what to do. No discussion. "Do it my way."
- **When to use**: Crisis situations, untrained staff, deadline is tight.
- **Pros**: Fast decisions. clear direction.
- **Cons**: Low morale, no creativity.

## 3. Laissez-Faire (Free-rein)

- **Style**: Leader stands back. "You handle it."
- **When to use**: Highly expert staff (e.g., Lawyers, Scientists).
- **Cons**: Chaos if staff are lazy.

## 4. Transactional

- **Style**: Reward and Punishment. "If you do X, I give you Y."

---

## Exam Tips

- **Identify Style**: Read the scenario carefully.
  - "Mr Jones asks his staff for input" -> Democratic.
  - "Ms Smith shouts orders and wants no feedback" -> Autocratic.
- **Recommendation**: Usually, Democratic is best, BUT if there is a crisis, Autocratic is acceptable.

---

## Quick Check

1. Which style is best for a fire in the building? (Autocratic).
2. Which style motivates staff the most? (Democratic).
3. What does "Laissez-Faire" mean? (Let them do / Leave alone).
