# The Average Clause

This applies when property is **Under-insured**.
(i.e., The Insured Value < The Real Market Value).

## The Rule

If you insure an item for less than it is worth, you are only covered for a *proportion* of the loss. You are your own insurer for the rest.

## The Formula

```
Claim Paid = (Amount Insured ÷ Actual Market Value) × Loss/Damage
```

## Worked Example

- **Market Value of House**: R1 000 000
- **Insured For**: R500 000 (Only 50% covered)
- **Fire Damage**: R200 000

**Calculation**:
`Claim = (500 000 ÷ 1 000 000) × 200 000`
`Claim = 0.5 × 200 000`
`Claim = R100 000`

*Result: Only R100 000 is paid. The owner loses the other R100 000.*

---

## Exam Tips

- **Goal**: Always apply the formula if you see "Under-insured".
- **Over-insurance**: If you insure for *more* than value, they still only pay the actual loss (Principle of Indemnity). You waste premiums.

## Quick Check

1. If you are under-insured, will the insurer pay the full claim? (No).
2. Calculate: Value R100k, Insured R80k, Loss R10k. (R8k paid).
3. Why does the Average Clause exist? (To force people to pay fair premiums for the full value).
