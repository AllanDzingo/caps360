# Insurance Concepts

## Compulsory vs Non-Compulsory

1. **Compulsory (By Law)**:
    - **UIF**: Unemployment Insurance Fund.
    - **RAF**: Road Accident Fund (Fuel levy).
    - **COIDA**: Compensation for Occupational Injuries and Diseases (Workplace).
2. **Non-Compulsory (Choice)**:
    - Fire, Theft, Life Insurance.

## Assurance vs Insurance

- **Assurance**: For events that *WILL* happen (e.g., Death, Retirement). Long-term. -> **Security**.
- **Insurance**: For events that *MIGHT* happen (e.g., Fire, Theft, Accident). Short-term. -> **Indemnity**.

## Principles of Insurance

1. **Indemnity**: To put you back in the same financial position as before the loss. You cannot make a profit from insurance.
2. **Insurable Interest**: You must have a financial interest in the item properly insured. (You can't insure your neighbour's car).
3. **Security**: Providing financial peace of mind for the future (Life assurance).

---

## Exam Tips

- **Confusion**: Students confuse Assurance and Insurance.
  - Assurance = Life (Certainty).
  - Insurance = Things (Possibility).
- **RAF**: Paid for by a levy on petrol/diesel, not a monthly premium by drivers.

## Quick Check

1. Is Life Cover Insurance or Assurance? (Assurance).
2. Name the fund that pays out if you are injured at work. (COIDA).
3. Can I insure a stranger's house? (No - No Insurable Interest).
