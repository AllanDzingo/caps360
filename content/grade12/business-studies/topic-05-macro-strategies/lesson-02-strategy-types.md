# Types of Business Strategies

## 1. Integration Strategies (Owning the supply chain)

- **Forward**: Buying your distributor/retailer. (e.g., Farm buys a Shop).
- **Backward**: Buying your supplier. (e.g., KFC buys a Chicken Farm).
- **Horizontal**: Buying a competitor. (e.g., Bank A buys Bank B).

## 2. Intensive Strategies (Working harder)

- **Market Penetration**: Selling *more* of existing product to existing market (e.g., Lower prices).
- **Market Development**: Selling existing product to a *new* market (e.g., Opening in a new country).
- **Product Development**: Selling a *new* product to existing market (e.g., Apple launches new iPhone).

## 3. Diversification Strategies (New things)

- **Concentric**: New product, related to current product. (Adding shoes to a clothing line).
- **Horizontal**: New product, unrelated, but to same customers. (Selling water bottles in a gym).
- **Conglomerate**: Totally new business. (Virgin Records starts Virgin Airlines).

## 4. Defensive Strategies (Saving the business)

- **Retrenchment**: Cutting jobs/expenses.
- **Divestiture**: Selling off part of the business assets.
- **Liquidation**: Closing down and selling everything to pay debts.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario ID**: This is the most common question. "Shoprite buys a farm." -> Backward Integration. "Shoprite opens a store in Nigeria." -> Market Development.
- **Horizontal Integration** vs **Horizontal Diversification**:
  - *Integration*: Buying a competitor (Same level).
  - *Diversification*: Adding a different product for same customers.

---

## Quick Check

1. If Apple buys a screen factory, is it Forward or Backward Integration? (Backward).
2. If Coca-Cola launches a new flavour, which Intensive strategy is this? (Product Development).
3. What is the "Last resort" strategy? (Liquidation).
