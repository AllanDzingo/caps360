# Porter's Five Forces

Used to analyse the **Market Environment** (The competition).

## The 5 Forces

### 1. Power of Suppliers

- **High**: If there is only one supplier (Monopoly). They can charge what they want.
- **Low**: If there are many suppliers. We can shop around.

### 2. Power of Buyers (Customers)

- **High**: If customers have many choices or buy in bulk. They can demand discounts.
- **Low**: If we sell a unique product.

### 3. Threat of Substitutes

- **High**: If customers can use something else (e.g., Butter vs Margarine).
- **Low**: If our product is essential (e.g., Electricity).

### 4. Threat of New Entrants

- **High**: If it's easy to start this business (Low barriers to entry). e.g., Car wash.
- **Low**: If it's expensive/hard to start. e.g., Airline.

### 5. Competitive Rivalry

- **High**: If there are many competitors fighting for market share.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Application**: You will be asked to apply Porter to a scenario.
- **Strategy**:
  - If Supplier Power is High: Strategy -> Backward Integration (Buy the supplier) or find new suppliers.
  - If Rivalry is High: Strategy -> Differentiation (Make product unique) or intensive marketing.

## Quick Check

1. If it costs R1 billion to start a business, is the "Threat of New Entrants" High or Low? (Low).
2. If there are 100 shops selling the same bread, is "Competitive Rivalry" High or Low? (High).
3. Which force deals with "Alternative products"? (Substitutes).
