# Investment Opportunities

## Types of Investments

### 1. JSE (Johannesburg Stock Exchange)

- A market where shares of public companies are traded.
- **Function**: Raises capital for companies and offers investment opportunities for the public.
- **Risks**: Share prices fluctuate (Market Risk).

### 2. Unit Trusts

- A fund manager pools money from many investors and invests it in a mix of shares, bonds, and property.
- **Pros**: Diversification (Don't put all eggs in one basket), Professional management.
- **Cons**: Management fees.

### 3. RSA Retail Savings Bonds

- Lending money to the Government.
- **Pros**: Very safe (Government guarantee), No fees, Good interest rate.
- **Cons**: Money is locked away for fixed period (2, 3, or 5 years).

### 4. Managed Portfolio

- An expert manages your specific portfolio of shares.

### 5. Shares vs Debentures

- **Shares**: You become an *owner*. You get Dividends (Variable). High risk.
- **Debentures**: You become a *lender*. You get Interest (Fixed). Lower risk.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**: "Granny has R10 000 and wants a safe investment." -> **RSA Retail Savings Bonds**.
- **Scenario**: "John wants to become a part-owner of Sasol." -> **Buy Shares on JSE**.
