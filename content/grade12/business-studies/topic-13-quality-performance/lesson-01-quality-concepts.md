# Quality Concepts

## Definition of Quality

- Conforming to requirements.
- Fitness for purpose.
- Getting it right the first time.

## 1. Quality Control (QC)

- **Focus**: The **Final Product**.
- **Action**: Inspecting, testing, or checking the product *after* it is made, but *before* it is sold.
- **Example**: Tasting a batch of biscuits at the end of the line.
- **Limitation**: It detects defects late (waste).

## 2. Quality Assurance (QA)

- **Focus**: The **Process**.
- **Action**: Ensuring systems are in place at *every stage* of production to prevent mistakes.
- **Example**: ISO 9000 standards. Checking ingredients *before* mixing.
- **Benefit**: Prevents defects (Zero defect goal).

---

## Exam Tips

- **Scenario**:
  - "Inspectors check the cars as they come off the assembly line." -> **Quality Control**.
  - "Suppliers are checked, staff are trained, and machines are serviced to ensure no errors occur." -> **Quality Assurance**.

---

## Quick Check

1. Which is "Proactive" (Preventing)? (Quality Assurance).
2. Which is "Reactive" (Checking)? (Quality Control).
3. What is the goal of QA? (Zero defects / Right first time).
