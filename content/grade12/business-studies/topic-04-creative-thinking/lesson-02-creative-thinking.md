# Problem Solving Techniques

You must know how to apply these to a scenario.

## 1. Brainstorming

- **Process**: Group generates as many ideas as possible. No judgment allowed.
- **Use**: When you need many new ideas quickly.

## 2. Delphi Technique

- **Process**: Ask experts. Send questionnaires anonymously. Summarize results and send back for re-voting.
- **Use**: When you need expert opinion without bringing them together (conflict avoidance or distance).

## 3. Nominal Group Technique

- **Process**: Everyone writes ideas silently. Then share one by one. Then vote/rank ideas.
- **Use**: To prevent one loud person from dominating the group.

## 4. Force Field Analysis

- **Process**: List "Driving Forces" (Positives) and "Restraining Forces" (Negatives).
- **Goal**: Strengthen the driving forces and weaken the restraining forces.
- **Use**: Implementing change.

---

## Exam Tips

- **Delphi**: Keyword is "Experts" and "Anonymous".
- **Force Field**: Keyword is "Change" and "Pros vs Cons".
- **Nominal**: Keyword is "Silent" to avoid dominance.
